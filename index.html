How Reconstitution Works
1
Your peptide vial contains lyophilized (freeze-dried) powder — e.g. 5mg of BPC-157.
2
You add BAC water to dissolve it. Adding 2ml to a 5mg vial = 2,500 mcg per ml.
3
A 100-unit insulin syringe holds 1ml total — 100 units = 1ml = 2,500 mcg in this example.
4
To inject 250 mcg: 250 ÷ 2,500 × 100 = 10 units on the syringe.
5
Always inject slowly into SubQ fat. Rotate sites. Keep reconstituted vials refrigerated.
